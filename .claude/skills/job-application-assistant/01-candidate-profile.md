## Professional Experience

### PhD Researcher - Bilkent University, UNAM (2022 - present)
Ankara, Turkey. Supervisor: Prof. Aykut Erbaş.
- Ran 100+ all-atom non-equilibrium MD simulations (LAMMPS, up to 100 ns each) on national HPC to resolve the microscopic origin of anomalous ionic conductivity in Na+-intercalated layered MnO2 (2D van der Waals solid)
- Demonstrated that applied fields drive spatial segregation of water into hydrated/dehydrated ionic domains, producing a collective ionic conduction regime that violates the Nernst-Einstein relation; decoupled morphology from hydration effects via frozen-layer control simulations
- Built the end-to-end Python analysis pipeline (NumPy, pandas, SciPy, NetworkX, OVITO) for a coarse-grained MD study of nuclear lamina self-assembly (1250-chain system, 22,500-bead elastic shell): KDTree-based network reconstruction, face-size extraction, SASA coverage metrics
- First author on a manuscript under review in *Physical Review Materials* (arXiv:2601.14836); second author on a bioRxiv 2026 preprint
- All simulation batches dispatched via SLURM on national HPC (TRUBA)

### MS Researcher - Bilkent University, UNAM (2019 - 2022)
Ankara, Turkey. Thesis: Molecular Dynamics Simulations Study of Rotaxane in Bulk and at Interfaces.
- First-author study (JPCB 2023) using all-atom MD (GROMACS) across seven protonation states to show pH-driven conformational switching kinetics of [5]rotaxane via CB6 ring shuttling; extracted switching timescales and free-energy barriers from exponential fits to SASA time traces and Kramers theory, validated across 20 replicas and two force fields
- Simulated polyrotaxane 2D network formation at a water-chloroform interface; characterised network porosity and charge-state-dependent lateral expansion
